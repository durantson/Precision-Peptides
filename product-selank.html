Anxiolytic Signaling Reagent

Selank is a synthetic analog of the tetrapeptide Tuftsin. It is researched for its anxiolytic (anti-anxiety) properties without the sedative or addictive side effects associated with traditional benzodiazepines. It functions by modulating the monoamine neurotransmitter systems and increasing BDNF expression.

Mechanistic & Immunological Context

GABAergic Pathway Interaction

Unlike standard GABA agonists, research suggests Selank modulates the GABAergic system through allosteric regulation of the GABA-A receptors, potentially influencing the binding affinity of natural GABA ligands.

Immunomodulatory Origins

Because Selank is derived from Tuftsin, it retains significant immunomodulatory properties. Studies focus on its ability to regulate cytokine balance, specifically IL-6 and the antiviral response in murine models.
