The Analytical Guide

Decoding High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) and Mass Spectrometry (MS) Reports.

Phase 01

HPLC: Purity Verification

HPLC is used to determine the **purity** of the peptide. It works by passing the sample through a column; different molecules travel at different speeds.

What to look for:

  • The Main Peak: This represents the target peptide. It should be sharp and dominant.
  • Integration Area: The "Area %" of the main peak relative to minor peaks determines the purity (e.g., 99.2%).
  • Baseline: A clean, flat baseline indicates the absence of systemic contamination.

Typical HPLC Chromatogram for >99% Purity

Phase 02

Mass Spec: Identity Confirmation

ESI-MS Spectrum confirming Molecular Mass

While HPLC shows purity, **Mass Spectrometry (MS)** proves the substance is actually the correct peptide by measuring its molecular weight.

What to look for:

  • Theoretical Mass: The calculated weight based on the amino acid sequence.
  • Observed Mass: The actual weight measured by the machine. These should match within a tiny margin (usually [M+H]+).
  • Peak Clusters: Represents the ionization of the molecule.
