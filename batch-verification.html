Understanding The Data

We don't just show you a number; we show you the physics. Here is how to interpret our Certificates of Analysis (COA).

Chromatography (HPLC)

Measures purity. The single tall peak represents the target peptide. Small bumps at the baseline indicate impurities. We reject anything with secondary peaks totaling >1%.

Mass Spectrometry (MS)

Measures identity. This confirms the molecular weight matches the theoretical formula (e.g., Semaglutide = 4113.58 Da). If the weight is off, the chemical is wrong.

Quantity Verification

We verify the actual net content. A "5mg" vial usually contains ~5.5mg of powder (peptide + counter-ions/water). We ensure the active peptide content meets the label claim.
