Targeted Lipolytic Fragment

AOD9604 is a stabilized analog of the lipolytic domain of human Growth Hormone. In laboratory models, it is investigated for its ability to stimulate fat metabolism (lipolysis) and inhibit lipogenesis without the growth-promoting or insulin-antagonistic effects associated with full-length hGH.

Mechanistic & Safety Profile

Beta-3 Adrenergic Interaction

Current research explores AOD9604's potential interaction with $\beta_3$-adrenergic receptors in white adipose tissue. This pathway is a primary focus for studies regarding thermogenesis and lipid oxidation without affecting systemic IGF-1 levels.

Chondrogenic Potential

Beyond lipolysis, AOD9604 is increasingly used in regenerative medicine models to study its effects on chondrocyte proliferation and proteoglycan synthesis in articular cartilage repair.
