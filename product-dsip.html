Neuromodulatory Profile

DSIP is a naturally occurring neuropeptide found in the hypothalamus, limbic system, and pituitary. In laboratory models, it is researched for its ability to induce delta-wave sleep and its potential to modulate the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, particularly cortisol levels.

Physiological Research Context

Endocrine Modulation

Current research utilizes DSIP to study the regulation of Luteinizing Hormone (LH) and Somatotropin secretion. It is observed to play a role in maintaining circadian homeostasis during periods of physiological stress.

Oxidative Stress & Protection

Studies explore DSIP's potential as an antioxidant in neuronal tissue. It is being investigated for its ability to reduce lipid peroxidation and protect mitochondrial function in various in vitro models.
