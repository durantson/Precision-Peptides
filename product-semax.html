Neuroprotective Reagent

Semax is a synthetic analog of the adrenocorticotropic hormone fragment ACTH(4-10). It is extensively studied for its neuroprotective and neurotrophic properties, specifically its ability to upregulate Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) and Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) in the brain.

Neurotrophic & Signaling Context

BDNF Expression Modulation

Research indicates that Semax induces a significant increase in mRNA expression of BDNF in the hippocampus. This pathway is a primary focus for studies regarding ischemic stroke recovery and cognitive enhancement modeling.

Enkephalin Degradation Inhibition

Semax is investigated for its role in inhibiting the enzymes responsible for the degradation of enkephalins. This mechanism is explored for its potential impact on neurotransmitter stability and antinociceptive research.
