Antimicrobial Mechanism

LL-37 is the only member of the cathelicidin family of antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) found in humans. It adopts an amphipathic alpha-helical structure in physiological conditions, allowing it to disrupt bacterial cell membranes through the "carpet model" or pore formation.

Research Applications

Wound Healing & Angiogenesis

Research indicates LL-37 promotes the proliferation of endothelial cells and fibroblasts. It is frequently investigated for its ability to induce tube formation in vascular modeling assays.

Immune Modulation

Beyond direct killing of pathogens, LL-37 acts as a chemotactic factor for neutrophils and T-cells, serving as a bridge between innate and adaptive immunity studies.
