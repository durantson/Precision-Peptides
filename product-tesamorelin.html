Primary Research Data

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analogue of human Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone (GHRH) comprised of 44 amino acids. The critical innovation in its structure is the addition of a trans-3-hexenoic acid group to the N-terminal tyrosine residue. This hydrophobic addition increases stability and extends the half-life compared to native GHRH. In clinical research models, Tesamorelin is widely studied for its high efficacy in reducing visceral adipose tissue (VAT) and improving lipid profiles (cholesterol/triglycerides) without altering glucose homeostasis significantly.
