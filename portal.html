Technical Knowledge Base

Verified laboratory protocols and molecular stability data for professional researchers.

What is the "Wall-Drip" method?
To prevent molecular denaturing, point the needle at the glass wall of the vial. Allow the Bacteriostatic Water to run down the glass into the lyophilized powder. **Never spray directly onto the powder**, as high-pressure impact can damage fragile peptide bonds.
Storage for reconstituted reagents?
Reconstituted peptides must be stored at 2°C – 8°C (36°F – 46°F). Avoid the refrigerator door due to temperature fluctuations. Once in solution, research integrity is typically maintained for 14-21 days.
Calculating mg per mL concentration?
Divide the total vial mg by the mL of water added.

Formula: 10mg Vial ÷ 2mL Water = 5mg/mL. On a standard U-100 syringe, 10 units = 0.5mg of reagent.
Can I shake the vial to mix?
No. Shaking causes "shearing stress," which breaks the delicate amino acid chains. Always use a gentle, circular swirling motion until the solution is completely clear.
Stability during transit heat?
Lyophilized (powder) peptides are extremely stable. They can withstand room-temperature exposure for up to 4 weeks during transit without degradation. Always refrigerate immediately upon arrival.
Significance of the vacuum seal?
The vacuum seal protects against oxidation. A "pull" on the syringe during reconstitution indicates an airtight, sterile environment. If no vacuum is present, the vial's integrity may be compromised.
