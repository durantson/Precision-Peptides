Copper Chelation & Bioactivity

GHK-Cu is a tripeptide with a high affinity for copper (II) ions. It is studied for its ability to regulate a wide array of human genes, particularly those involved in copper-dependent enzymatic processes, collagen synthesis, and tissue remodeling.

Analytical & Genomic Context

Extracellular Matrix (ECM) Modeling

Research indicates that GHK-Cu stimulates the synthesis of collagen, dermatan sulfate, and chondroitin sulfate. It is frequently utilized in in-vitro models to study the modulation of metalloproteinases (MMPs) and their inhibitors (TIMPs).

Anti-Oxidant Properties

GHK-Cu has been observed to inhibit lipid peroxidation and neutralize toxic products of oxidative stress. Its role in quenching reactive carbonyl species is a primary focus for longevity and anti-aging research.
