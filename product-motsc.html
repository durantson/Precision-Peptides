MOTS-c

Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the 12S rRNA-c

Biochemical Profile

MOTS-c is a 16-amino acid peptide encoded by the mitochondrial genome rather than the nuclear genome. It functions as a signaling molecule that translocates to the nucleus in response to metabolic stress, acting as a critical regulator of insulin sensitivity and metabolic homeostasis.

Researchers utilize MOTS-c in studies regarding the AMPK pathway, investigating its ability to promote glucose uptake and enhance fatty acid oxidation. Its unique mitochondrial origin makes it a primary subject for "mitokine" research.
