Technical Knowledge Base

Verified laboratory protocols, reconstitution physics, and molecular stability data.

What is the "Wall-Drip" reconstitution method?
To prevent molecular denaturing (breaking the peptide bonds via kinetic force), point the needle at the glass wall of the vial. Allow the bacteriostatic water to run down the glass rather than spraying directly onto the lyophilized powder.
Optimal storage for reconstituted liquid?
Once mixed, the peptide is less stable. It must be stored at 2°C – 8°C (refrigerated). Avoid the door of the refrigerator due to frequent temperature fluctuations. Utilization should occur within 14-21 days for maximum potency.
How do I calculate concentration?
Concentration = (Total mg in vial) / (mL of water added).

Example: A 10mg vial + 2mL of water = 5mg/mL.

Why is there a vacuum seal?
Vials are vacuum-sealed at the factory to prevent oxidation and ensure sterility. A strong vacuum pull (sucking in the water) during reconstitution is a primary indicator of factory seal integrity.
